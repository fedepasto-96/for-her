💊 Your current stack

What you're taking right now — keep these going until the bottles run out. 💛

Omega 3-6-9
What it does: heart, brain & skin; calms inflammation and supports hormones. Finishing your current bottle, then switching (see the new plan below).
Vitamin C — Ester-C 500mg
What it does: antioxidant & immune support, and the cofactor that lets your body build collagen. Finishing it up. ~3 months in — a good run. Collagen takes its place next.
Biotin 5000mcg on now
What it does: a B-vitamin for hair strength & nail growth. Working well for you. You're planning a 3-month break once this bottle's done — just message me when you stop and I'll move it. (Also: pause it a few days before any blood test — it can skew thyroid & cardiac labs.)
Vitamin D3 — 2 caps/week
What it does: bones, immune system & mood; pairs with K2 to steer calcium into bone, not arteries. Totally safe at this dose — it works out to ~1000 IU/day, on the gentle side. Fine for maintenance, but many women feel better a bit higher. No need to change blind: a quick blood test tells us whether to stay here or nudge up to 2000-4000/day (with K2). 👍
Magnesium glycinate 200mg keep
What it does: sleep, muscle recovery, calm & easing PMS. Can go up to 300mg.
Creatine 5g keep
What it does: strength & muscle gain, plus a little mental focus. Top pick for muscle gain. Consistency matters more than timing.
Whey protein keep
What it does: a complete protein to build & repair muscle. Your main one — 1 scoop (~20-25g) post-workout or whenever you're short for the day.
Clear protein keep
What it does: lighter, juice-like whey isolate — same muscle support, easier on the stomach. Use it on days whey feels too heavy.
D-Mannose + Cranberry as-needed
What it does: urinary-tract support — helps keep bacteria from sticking. Really a UTI tool — skip the daily dose unless you get recurrent UTIs.

What it does: skin elasticity & hydration, plus hair, nails and joints. Collagen peptides (~10g/day). Keep a little vitamin C with it — see the note below.

Vitamin D3 → test & likely raise test
Check your levels, then move to 2000-4000 IU/day paired with K2.
Add: Ferritin / iron test new
What it does: carries oxygen in the blood — supports energy & hair. Lean + a monthly cycle often means low iron. Test, don't guess.
Add: Methylfolate 400mcg new
What it does: active folate for hormonal balance now, and groundwork for fertility.
Add: Women's multivitamin new
What it does: a safety net that gently fills any gaps from intermittent fasting + a lean intake.
Vitamin C + collagen — do you need both? Not two big doses, no. But they're partners, not duplicates: vitamin C is the cofactor your body requires to turn collagen into actual skin and joint tissue. So the sweet spot is collagen + a small amount of vitamin C (a combined powder, or ~250-500mg C on the side). If you eat plenty of fruit & veg you may already get enough — the separate high-dose tablet isn't essential once you're on collagen.

Fitness Plan 💪

Simple, strength-first, and built for lean muscle & long-term health — not punishment.

💪 Your training plan

3 strength sessions + movement. Lift in the 8-12 rep range, add a little weight over time, and rest 60-90s between sets.

💪 Lower body
Glutes & legsSquats · hip thrusts · Romanian deadlifts · lunges · calf raises
💪 Upper body
Back, shoulders & armsRows · lat pulldown · shoulder press · push-ups · bicep curls
💪 Full body
A bit of everythingDeadlift · goblet squat · chest press · band walks · plank
🚶‍♀️ Cardio
Heart & recovery30-40 min brisk walk, light jog, cycle or dance — keep it easy & enjoyable
🧘‍♀️ Mobility
Flexibility & calm20-30 min yoga or stretching — great for sleep, stress & recovery
The golden rules 💕 — progress slowly (a little heavier or one more rep each week), eat enough protein to actually build muscle, and never skip sleep. Strong is the goal, not small.
The two biggest levers aren't pills 💛 — hit your protein target, and nudge sleep from 6-7h toward 7.5-8h. Those move muscle gain and hormones more than any capsule. When you're ~3 months from trying to conceive, swap the methylfolate for a full prenatal.

General wellness information, made with love — not medical advice. Please confirm vitamin D, iron, and any new supplements with your doctor.
